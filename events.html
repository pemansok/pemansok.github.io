Soundbox


THAMES is working with Spitalfields Music, Drake Music and the London Symphony Orchestra to bring East London’s only all-ability inclusive ensemble to the Saturday Music Centre. This will see up to 20 students aged 14-25 come together using a mixture of traditional instruments, music technology and singing to experiment with new sounds and styles through group creative composition and song-writing, improvisation, skills exploration and ensemble playing. It is an integrated ensemble for both disabled and non-disabled musicians.


Prior to the full launch in January 2018, there will be 2 taster sessions this term on 11th and 25th November. Students involved will take part in sessions at SMC, 10.45-11.15 and 11.30-1 (a small extension to their usual morning). Those interested and selected to be part of the main project will work with Soundbox on 4 SMC mornings in the Spring term. This will complement their current SMC timetable. 


There are limited places for this opportunity. If your child is aged 14+ and is interested in joining this new and exciting ensemble please let me know by Friday 13th October. 


For further information, please contact me via email.

‘A Brief History of the Coolest Instrument in the World’


Led by musician Chris Montague, A Brief History of the Coolest Instrument in the World is a performance which marks the milestones of the electric guitar’s prolific role through 80 years of popular culture, from Charlie Christian to Chuck Berry, Jimi Hendrix and beyond. This unique show highlights the great guitarists through the ages with through a mixture of performance and speech, celebrating the versatility of the instrument and providing an opportunity for amateur guitarists to perform an original composition with Chris and his band on stage.


Open to all guitarists, this project will see musicians come together to perform as part of the EFG London Jazz Festival. There is a rehearsal on the evening of the 25th October and a performance on Saturday 18th November (afternoon). 


Please note that students should not miss SMC to attend this event
